QUESTION 5

Difficulty (F / I / A): f
Priority (M / S / G): m

Stem: Explain how the force-velocity relationship arises from the underlying cross-bridge biophysics, and why it explains the design observation that "fast" muscles have high Vmax but lower endurance.

Model answer: The force-velocity curve plots shortening velocity (v) against external load (F). It is hyperbolic: v = Vmax at zero load, falls progressively as load rises, and reaches zero at the maximum isometric tension P0 (where the load equals the most the muscle can generate). Hill's equation: (P + a)(v + b) = (P0 + a)b describes it.

QUESTION 15

Difficulty (F / I / A): i
Priority (M / S / G): s

Stem: Common misconception: "Strength training builds muscle by increasing the number of muscle fibres." Critique this and explain the actual cellular mechanisms of training-induced hypertrophy.

Model answer: In adult mammals, including humans, strength training increases muscle size almost entirely by **hypertrophy** (more contractile protein per fibre), not by **hyperplasia** (more fibres). Some splitting of fibres has been demonstrated experimentally in extreme conditions, but this is a minor contribution to physiological adult training adaptations.
