QUESTION 6

Difficulty (F / I / A): i
Priority (M / S / G): s

Stem: Describe the role of voltage-dependent Mg²⁺ block of the NMDA receptor in synaptic plasticity. Why is the NMDA receptor described as a "coincidence detector"?

Model answer: At resting membrane potentials, the pore of the NMDA receptor is occluded by an extracellular Mg²⁺ ion. Glutamate binding alone is therefore insufficient to allow current flow. When the postsynaptic membrane is sufficiently depolarised — typically by AMPA-receptor-mediated current from concurrent strong synaptic input — the Mg²⁺ leaves the pore. Only then, with glutamate (and the obligatory co-agonist glycine or D-serine) bound and the membrane depolarised, does the NMDA channel open and pass Na⁺, K⁺, and importantly Ca²⁺.
