The two principal ascending somatosensory pathways tested throughout this chapter are summarised first for reference. The questions then probe the same anatomy from different angles.

**Dorsal column–medial lemniscal pathway (DCML)**:

- Sensations carried: **fine (discriminative) touch, vibration, conscious proprioception, two-point discrimination, kinaesthesia**.
- Fibre type: large myelinated (Aβ).
- 1st-order neurone: **dorsal root ganglion** (pseudo-unipolar). Central process ascends ipsilaterally in the dorsal column to the medulla.
- 2nd-order neurone: **gracile or cuneate nucleus** in the medulla. Axon then **decussates** at the medulla as the internal arcuate fibres → medial lemniscus.
- 3rd-order neurone: **VPL of thalamus** → primary somatosensory cortex (S1).
- Decussation: **medulla** [Guyton ch.48].

**Anterolateral system (ALS)**:

- Components: **lateral spinothalamic** (pain, temperature), **anterior spinothalamic** (crude touch, pressure, tickle, itch), **spinoreticular**, **spinotectal**, **spinomesencephalic** tracts.
- Fibre type: small myelinated (Aδ) and unmyelinated (C).
- 1st-order neurone: **dorsal root ganglion**. Central process enters the dorsal horn.
- 2nd-order neurone: **dorsal horn** of spinal cord (laminae I, IV, V). Axon **decussates** at the spinal cord segmental level (within 1–2 segments of entry) via the anterior white commissure.
- 3rd-order neurone: **VPL of thalamus** → S1 cortex.
- Decussation: **spinal cord** [Guyton ch.48] [Guyton ch.49].

QUESTION 1

Stem: Which of the following is not a component of the anterolateral system?

Correct answer: Dorsal column

Explanation: The **anterolateral system** comprises the spinothalamic tracts (anterior and lateral), spinoreticular, spinotectal, spinomesencephalic and spinohypothalamic tracts — all carrying small-fibre (Aδ, C) information including pain, temperature, crude touch, itch and tickle [Guyton ch.48] [Guyton ch.49]. The **dorsal column** is the ascending tract of the _dorsal column–medial lemniscal_ pathway, completely separate from the anterolateral system. Hence (d).

Hints:

1. Recall the components of the anterolateral system.
2. The dorsal column is the named tract of the _other_ major somatosensory pathway.
3. The exception is the ascending tract of the DCML.

Distractors:

- "Anterior spinothalamic tract" — Component of ALS (crude touch).
- "Lateral spinothalamic tract" — Component of ALS (pain, temperature).
- "Spinotectal tract" — Component of ALS.

QUESTION 4

Stem: Which of the following is true regarding the dorsal column pathway?

Correct answer: Decussation occurs at medulla

Explanation: DCML organisation [Guyton ch.48]: **1st-order neurone — DRG** (not dorsal horn). **2nd-order neurone — gracile/cuneate nucleus in the medulla** (not thalamus). Axons decussate in the medulla as the **internal arcuate fibres** → medial lemniscus → **3rd-order neurone in VPL of thalamus**. The pathway carries **fine touch, vibration, proprioception** (not pain/temperature). Hence (c).

Hints:

1. Trace the three neurones of the DCML pathway and their locations.
2. The decussation occurs after the 2nd-order neurone, in the medulla.
3. The exception that is correct is the medullary decussation.

Distractors:

- "First order neuron is in the dorsal horn of spinal cord" — Wrong: 1st-order is in the DRG.
- "Second order neuron is in the thalamus" — Wrong: 2nd-order is in the gracile/cuneate nucleus; thalamus hosts the 3rd-order.
- "It is concerned with pain and temperature" — Wrong: DCML carries fine touch, vibration, proprioception.

QUESTION 5

Stem: Which of the following is true regarding anterolateral pathway?

Correct answer: Second order neuron is in the dorsal horn of spinal cord

Explanation: ALS organisation [Guyton ch.48]: 1st-order neurone in **DRG** (option (a) is also true!); 2nd-order neurone in **dorsal horn** (laminae I, IV, V) — **decussates at spinal cord** (segmentally within 1–2 levels) via anterior white commissure. The keyed answer (d) emphasises the dorsal-horn 2nd-order neurone — the most distinguishing feature of ALS compared to DCML. Option (a) "First order neuron is in DRG" is _also_ true (both pathways have 1st-order neurones in DRG), but the question key prefers (d). Option (b) is false (ALS decussates in spinal cord, not medulla). Option (c) is false (ALS carries pain/temperature/crude touch, not fine touch/proprioception).

Hints:

1. Identify the location of the 2nd-order neurone of the ALS — it is the key distinction from DCML.
2. ALS 2nd-order is in the dorsal horn; DCML 2nd-order is in the medulla.
3. The decussation of ALS occurs at the spinal cord level.

Distractors:

- "First order neuron is in the dorsal root ganglion" — Defensible (also true), but (d) is the more distinguishing feature.
- "Decussation occurs at medulla" — Wrong: ALS decussates in the spinal cord.
- "It is concerned with fine touch and proprioception" — Wrong: that is DCML.

[AMBIGUITY NOTE: Option (a) is *also* a true statement about the ALS — both DCML and ALS have 1st-order neurones in the DRG. The keyed answer (d) is the more specific/distinguishing feature, but a careful student could defensibly choose (a).]

QUESTION 7

Stem: Which of the following is true regarding the anterolateral pathway?

Correct answer: Fibres cross in the spinal cord

Explanation: ALS fibres decussate at the **spinal cord** level — within 1–2 segments of entering the cord, via the anterior white commissure [Guyton ch.48] [Guyton ch.49]. This is the _opposite_ of the DCML, which decussates at the medulla. Hence (b). Option (a) reverses modalities; (c) confuses ALS with its opposite; (d) misplaces decussation.

- "It carries fine touch and proprioception" — Wrong: that is DCML.

QUESTION 8

Stem: Which of the following tracts carry pain and temperature sensation?

Correct answer: Anterolateral system

Explanation: **Pain and temperature** are carried by the **lateral spinothalamic tract**, which is the major component of the **anterolateral system** [Guyton ch.49]. **Dorsal column** carries fine touch/vibration/proprioception. **Spinocerebellar tracts** carry unconscious proprioception to the cerebellum. **Corticospinal tract** is _descending motor_, not ascending sensory.

Hints:

1. Recall which ascending pathway carries pain and temperature.
2. Pain and temperature use small fibres (Aδ, C) and the spinothalamic tract.
3. The spinothalamic tract is part of the ALS.

Distractors:

- "Dorsal column" — Wrong (carries fine touch, vibration, proprioception).
- "Spinocerebellar tract" — Wrong (unconscious proprioception to cerebellum).
- "Corticospinal tract" — Wrong (descending motor).

QUESTION 9

Stem: Which of the following is the first relay station for the dorsal column-medial lemniscus pathway?

Correct answer: Gracile and cuneate nuclei

Explanation: The DCML pathway's **first synapse** (relay station) is at the **gracile (medial, lower body) and cuneate (lateral, upper body) nuclei in the dorsal medulla** — this is where the 2nd-order neurone resides [Guyton ch.48]. The 1st-order neurone (in DRG) does not synapse in the spinal cord — it ascends ipsilaterally in the dorsal column directly to these medullary nuclei. The thalamus is the _second_ relay station (3rd-order neurone). "Medulla" alone is too imprecise — the specific nuclei are the gracile and cuneate.

Hints:

1. Recall where the DCML 1st-order axon first synapses.
2. The 2nd-order neurone is in the lower medulla.
3. Two paired nuclei — one for lower body, one for upper body — are the answer.

Distractors:

- "Thalamus" — Second relay (3rd-order neurone).
- "Dorsal horn of spinal cord" — Wrong: that's the ALS first synapse.
- "Medulla" — Imprecise (the specific nuclei are gracile and cuneate).

QUESTION 14

Stem: Decussation of fibres in the dorsal column-medial lemniscus pathway occurs at the level of:

Correct answer: Medulla

Explanation: DCML fibres decussate at the **medulla** as the internal arcuate fibres — axons of 2nd-order neurones in the gracile/cuneate nuclei cross to form the contralateral medial lemniscus, which ascends through pons and midbrain to the VPL of the thalamus [Guyton ch.48].

Hints:

1. Recall the level of DCML decussation.
2. DCML crosses at the medulla, ALS at the spinal cord.
3. The crossing is performed by the 2nd-order neurones' axons.

Distractors:

- "Spinal cord" — Wrong (that is ALS).
- "Pons" — Wrong (DCML axons traverse pons but cross in medulla).
- "Midbrain" — Wrong.

QUESTION 16

Stem: The first order neuron of the dorsal column pathway is located in:

Correct answer: Dorsal root ganglion

Explanation: All primary somatosensory afferents have their cell bodies (1st-order neurones) in the **dorsal root ganglion** (or trigeminal ganglion for the head) [Guyton ch.48]. Both DCML and ALS share this 1st-order neurone location; they differ in their 2nd-order locations.

Hints:

1. Recall the location of 1st-order somatosensory neurones.
2. Pseudo-unipolar primary afferents have their somata in the DRG.
3. The DRG is the 1st-order location for both DCML and ALS.

Distractors:

- "Dorsal horn of spinal cord" — 2nd-order for ALS, not 1st-order anywhere.
- "Gracile and cuneate nuclei" — 2nd-order for DCML.
- "Thalamus" — 3rd-order for both.

QUESTION 19

Stem: Which of the following is the site of decussation for the anterolateral pathway?

Correct answer: Spinal cord

Explanation: The ALS decussates at the **spinal cord** segmental level — within 1–2 segments of dorsal-root entry, via the anterior white commissure [Guyton ch.48] [Guyton ch.49]. This early crossing is the basis of clinical signs in cord lesions: a hemicord lesion (Brown-Séquard syndrome) causes contralateral loss of pain/temperature (ALS, already decussated below the lesion) and ipsilateral loss of fine touch/vibration/proprioception (DCML, not yet decussated).

Hints:

1. Recall where the ALS crosses.
2. Crossing occurs early — in the spinal cord, not the medulla.
3. This early crossing underlies Brown-Séquard syndrome.

Distractors:

- "Medulla" — Wrong (that is DCML).
- "Thalamus" — No decussation occurs there.
- "Pons" — No decussation occurs there for somatosensory pathways.

QUESTION 20

Stem: The third order neuron of both dorsal column and anterolateral pathways is located in:

Correct answer: Thalamus

Explanation: Both DCML and ALS converge on the **ventral posterolateral (VPL) nucleus of the thalamus** for their 3rd-order neurones, which then project to the **primary somatosensory cortex (S1)** in the postcentral gyrus [Guyton ch.48]. Trigeminal sensory pathways converge at the **VPM** of the thalamus instead.

Hints:

1. Recall the convergence point of both somatosensory pathways before cortex.
2. The 3rd-order neurone is the thalamic relay.
3. The VPL of the thalamus is the answer.

Distractors:

- "Dorsal root ganglion" — 1st-order.
- "Dorsal horn of spinal cord" — 2nd-order for ALS.
- "Gracile and cuneate nuclei" — 2nd-order for DCML.
